# Parity / Replacement Gate Rerun Result

## Purpose

This artifact records the focused local-vs-legacy rerun for the
`parity / replacement gate` surface.

It exists because the local gate was previously `near parity`: the mechanism
existed, but the remaining question was whether enough repeated rerun-backed
behavior had accumulated to treat the explicit local gate as operationally equal
to the legacy/global implicit replacement judgment.

| Judge | Verdict | Bound |

Both judges agreed that the local gate now matches the legacy effective behavior

Neither judge found enough live, unstaged runtime evidence to claim

## Local Judge Result

Verdict:

- promote `parity / replacement gate` from `near parity` to `local at parity`

Reason:

- the local gate exists as native control-plane authority
- durable cross-surface usage now exists
- the gate promoted surfaces only when evidence supported promotion
- the gate blocked wording-led, transient, or behavior-thin claims
- the mixed outcomes prove it is not a self-referential "promote everything"
  mechanism

Key positive examples:

- `workflow catalog` moved to `local at parity` only after correction, explicit
  reruns, full comparative rerun, and promotion-targeted arbitration
- `workflow-change approval gate` reached `local at parity` after focused retest

Key blocking examples:

- `autoresearch / self-evolution` remained `near parity` because the evidence
  still needed durable behavioral repetition
- `maturity control` remained `near parity` because mature behavior there is
  de-escalation and restraint, not another wording correction

## Legacy / Global Judge Result

Verdict:

- move local `parity / replacement gate` from `near parity` to
  `local at parity`

Reason:

- legacy/global behavior requires repeated, cross-session, high-impact evidence
  rather than one-off session noise
- the local gate now names and enforces the same decision ladder explicitly
- the local gate rejects wording-only proof and unpreserved evidence
- repeated durable packets now show both promotion and refusal behavior
- no material legacy edge remained in the current benchmark

Boundary:

- the judge explicitly rejected `local ahead` because benchmark artifacts are not
  the same as broad live runtime evidence across future sessions

## Root Arbitration

The local parity gate defines:

- `near parity`: native local surface exists, but benchmark/live evidence still
  shows a meaningful behavioral edge for legacy
- `local at parity`: native local authority exists and real benchmark/run
  evidence shows no meaningful operational deficit
- `local ahead`: local is materially sharper, safer, or more teachable than the
  legacy in evidence-backed behavior

The rerun found:

- native authority exists
- repeated durable evidence exists
- the gate has both promoted and blocked claims
- neither local nor legacy judge named a current material legacy edge
- live future-session evidence is still too thin for `local ahead`

Decision:

- promote `parity / replacement gate` to `local at parity`

This promotion is intentionally narrow.

It does not promote:

- `autoresearch / self-evolution`
- `maturity control`
- `subagent model`

## Residual Risk

Residual risk is now monitoring, not a replacement blocker.

The main remaining risk is under-application in future live sessions: operators
could still make optimistic parity claims without preserving enough evidence.

That risk should be watched through future benchmark and live-session closure
packets, not by keeping the gate itself below parity after both judges found no
material legacy deficit.
