# Parity Decision: Persisted-Modeling Review

## Purpose

This artifact is the explicit `parity-replacement-gate` packet for the
`persisted-modeling review` surface.

It turns the benchmark outcome into an auditable replacement decision instead
of leaving the judgment distributed across the battery memory, harvest plan,
and gap matrix.

## Surface Under Judgment

- local native surface:
  - `core/review/persisted-modeling-forensics.md`
  - `core/review/persisted-modeling-defect-bias.md`
- legacy comparison surface:
  - legacy/global `accelerate` persisted-modeling review behavior as exercised
    in the comparative Prop4You backend battery

## Why This Surface Matters

This surface governs whether `accelerate` can:

- review relational / persisted-modeling shapes usefully
- recover scope honestly when the requested literal target is absent
- reconcile doctrine, persistence, and runtime behavior
- rank directly actionable defects ahead of abstract architectural smell

It matters because this was one of the last benchmark classes where the legacy
runtime kept a practical edge after the local had already become stronger in
structure and doctrine.

## Benchmark Or Run Evidence

### Evidence Family

- real comparative benchmark battery
- repeated reruns against the same Prop4You backend target
- benchmark memory preserved from `R1` through `R6`

### Evolution Of The Evidence

- `R1` through `R3` showed the local below legacy because it still lagged on:
  - semantic recovery behavior
  - then `dual scope` behavior
- `R4` showed the local fixing the scope-handling gap and winning the
  scope-class rerun
- `R5` showed the remaining deficit had narrowed to:
  - concrete domain-defect aggression
- `R6` reran the battery after the native defect-bias supplement and a bounded
  rerun contract

### Decisive `R6` Evidence

`R6` is the decisive parity packet for this surface because:

- the local stayed behaviorally in real `dual scope`
- the rerun was bounded to explicit source files after a permissive attempt was
  aborted for drifting into derived artifacts
- the local moved quickly into invariant-first review instead of staying at
  doctrine-only critique
- the local surfaced directly actionable defect classes that were previously a
  legacy advantage, including:
  - the impossible active uniqueness condition on `SavedProperty`
  - persisted-identity vs runtime-lookup mismatch review
  - router / write-guard coverage review
  - BaseModel / provenance / truth-ownership checks

The legacy remained useful and methodologically strong, but in the same bounded
rerun window it was slower and more setup-heavy and did not beat the local on
the decisive defect-capture axis.

## Residual Gap Statement

Residual gap for this surface:

- no material operational deficit remains for the local on this battery class

Residual difference that still exists:

- the legacy is still a valuable harvest source for future review surfaces and
  for domain intuition in unfamiliar schemas
- that does not currently amount to a stronger primary method for this
  persisted-modeling surface

## Replacement Risk

The main risk is over-generalization.

This packet proves replacement readiness for:

- `persisted-modeling review`

It does **not** by itself prove that every review branch or every control-plane
surface is now `local ahead`.

The safe reading is:

- this surface can be treated as locally superior
- broader replacement claims must still be made surface by surface

## Decision Proposal

- verdict: `local ahead`

Effect:

- the local native persisted-modeling review surface can now be treated as the
  primary operational authority
- the legacy/global runtime becomes supporting doctrine and harvest source for
  this surface, not the primary method

## Follow-On Consequence

Future comparative work should now spend less time re-proving this surface and
more time using it as:

- a benchmarked local strength
- a template for parity decisions on adjacent review surfaces
