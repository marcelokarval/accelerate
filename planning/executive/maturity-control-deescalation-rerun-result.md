## Scenario

new risk:

- too many benchmark-specific rules
- repeated reruns whose main purpose was to force promotion
- possible workflow overfitting instead of mature simplification

The operator question was whether maturity control should:

- simplify the loop
- promote the surface
- mutate maturity-control doctrine
- or keep the current surface unchanged

## What Maturity Control Requires Now

Maturity control should de-escalate the convergence loop:

- stop adding benchmark-specific rules unless repeated structural failure is
  proven
- preserve useful correction evidence durably only when it affects parity,
  promotion, workflow truth, or closure
- route durable learnings to the smallest correct home
- avoid growing maturity-control itself for this scenario
- use future natural parity or promotion cycles to prove behavior

## Promotion Decision

Do not promote from `near parity`.

Do not mutate `core/workflows/maturity-control.md`.

Do not create another benchmark-specific rule.
